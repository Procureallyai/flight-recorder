# Disciplined Frontier Essay Style

Use this style for public-facing essays, Devpost narrative, README product narrative, founder commentary, strategic notes, and video scripts.

## Voice

Write with serious founder-operator judgement, technical fluency, philosophical clarity, and commercial realism. Treat governance as operational infrastructure. Avoid generic marketing, academic density, legal overreach, hype, casual slang, and unsupported certainty.

## Argument

1. Open with a compressed thesis when appropriate.
2. Ground the argument in a concrete engineering, buyer, reviewer, audit, or operational context.
3. Identify the weak assumption or distorted incentive.
4. Make a clean distinction between real and performative practice.
5. Explain second-order consequences.
6. Connect the point to evidence, verification, auditability, controls, procurement trust, or serious artificial intelligence deployment.
7. End with a durable principle, not a slogan.

## Product claims

- Prefer: “Flight Recorder helps teams produce and verify evidence for serious agentic software engineering.”
- Use precise terms such as evidence, verification, auditability, controls, decision records, traceability, assurance, reviewable systems, and operational discipline.
- Do not claim legal approval, certification, guaranteed compliance, guaranteed correctness, OpenAI endorsement, or full conformity.

## Mechanics

- Preserve United Kingdom English spelling and the Oxford comma.
- Avoid em dashes.
- Expand shortened terms on first use when the audience may not know them.
- Use short declarative openings and focused paragraphs.
- Avoid unsupported numerical claims and copied source phrasing.

## Final quality pass

- Clear thesis.
- Meaningful distinction.
- Concrete operational example.
- Second-order consequence.
- No hype or unsupported regulatory claim.
- Product wording matches shipped evidence.
- Ending lands on a durable principle.
